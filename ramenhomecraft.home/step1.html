ekstra Broth Flavors:
 Dashi, a Japanese stock made from ingredients like bonito flakes and dried kelp, can be used for shoyu and shio ramen.
Miso paste (fermented soybeans) is used to add depth to miso ramen.
Soy sauce, especially dark soy sauce, is used in shoyu ramen.
Kombu (dried kelp) is used for a vegetarian or vegan ramen base.

 To Enhance Flavor:
 Ginger and garlic slices are added to infuse flavors.
Onions and mirin (a sweet rice wine) can add sweetness.
Sake (rice wine) may be used for complexity.
Sesame Paste or Oil can be used to create a nutty and rich flavor in some ramen broths.

Chicken-Based Broth:
 Chicken bones (like carcasses and wings) are simmered to create a creamy and rich broth for chicken ramen. Or cheat and just use a half chicken bouillon cube

 Pork-Based Broth (Tonkotsu):
 Pork bones (like femurs and trotters) are boiled for a long time to make a creamy and milky broth, which forms the base for tonkotsu ramen.

 beef-Based Broth:
 Beef bones (such as marrow or knuckle bones) are simmered to create a creamy and rich broth for beef ramen. Or cheat and just use a half beef bouillon cube

 Vegan/Vegetarian Broth:
 A vegetable stock made from ingredients like mushrooms, carrots, and onions is used for plant-based ramen. Or cheat and just use a half vegatables bouillon cube

When you picked your flavor. Bring a large pot, heat a bit of oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic, chopped onion, and sliced ginger.
Pour your choosen broth and add soy sauce, dashi or miso paste. If you're using dried mushrooms or kombu, add them at this stage.
The key is to simmer these ingredients together to extract their flavors and create a tasty and balanced broth for your ramen. Feel free to mix and match these ingrediens to create your own signature ramen bowl.
Remember to season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the soy sauce for the desired saltiness.
