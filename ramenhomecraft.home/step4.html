Ramen bowls can be customized with a variety of vegetables to add flavor, texture, and nutritional value. The choice of vegetables often depends on personal preference and the type of ramen you're making. Here are some of the most common vegetables used in ramen:

Step 4 - CHOOSE YOUR FAVORITE Vegatables

Scallions (Green Onions) These are a staple in most ramen bowls. They add a fresh, oniony flavor and a pleasant crunch. You can use both the green and white parts, typically sliced diagonally.

Bok Choy
 Bok choy is a type of Chinese cabbage with tender leaves and crunchy stalks. It's often halved or quartered and blanched before being added to ramen.

Bean Sprouts
 Bean sprouts provide a nice crunch and a mild, nutty flavor. They are commonly found in ramen, especially in tonkotsu ramen.

Garlic
Minced or sliced garlic can be sautéed and used as a garnish or flavor enhancer in ramen.

Spinach
 Baby spinach leaves or mature spinach can be blanched and added to ramen for a burst of green and earthy notes.

Red Bell Peppers
 Sliced red bell peppers add sweetness and vibrant color to ramen. They are commonly used in vegetable-based ramen.

Corn Kernels
 Sweet corn adds a touch of natural sweetness and color contrast to the broth. It's a popular addition, especially in miso ramen.

Ginger
 Sliced or julienned ginger can be added to the broth or used as a topping for extra zing.

Seaweed (Nori)
 Nori sheets are sometimes placed on top of the ramen or crumbled into it. They impart a subtle sea flavor and an enjoyable texture.

Carrots
 Julienne or thinly sliced carrots can add a slight sweetness and a pop of color to your ramen bowl.

Mushrooms
 Sliced or whole mushrooms like shiitake, enoki, or wood ear mushrooms can be sautéed and added to ramen for their earthy flavor and meaty texture.

Cabbage
 Thinly sliced cabbage can provide a satisfying crunch and mild cabbage flavor. It's often used in ramen salads.

Hope you got some inspiration with the vegatables! Feel free to mix and match these vegetables to create your own signature ramen bowl. The combination of vegetables you choose can complement the flavor profile of your broth and add a delightful visual appeal to your dish.
